📋 Answer Key — With Explanations (For Teachers & Students)
Section A: Grammar (Q1–10)
1.B—"have" — Present Perfect is used with "never" to talk about life experience up to now. "I have never visited…"
2.C—"had started" — Past Perfect is used for an action that was already completed before another past action ("by the time we arrived").
3.B—"was built" — Passive Voice (Past Simple). The subject "bridge" did not build itself; it was built by someone.
4.A—"study" — First Conditional: If + Present Simple, … will + base verb. The if-clause must use the present tense, not "will."
5.A—"who" — Use "who" as a relative pronoun when referring to a person who is the subject of the relative clause.
6.C—"must not" — "Must not" expresses a strong prohibition (it is not allowed). "Should not" is weaker advice.
7.B—"but" — "But" is a conjunction showing contrast between two opposite ideas: he works hard, yet earns little.
8.D—"the highest" — Superlative form requires "the" + adjective + -est. "Most tallest" is incorrect (double superlative).
9.C—"will be taking" — Future Continuous describes an action in progress at a specific time in the future ("at 9 o'clock tomorrow").
10.A—"are" — With "Neither … nor …," the verb agrees with the subject closest to it. "brothers" is plural → use "are."
Section B: Vocabulary (Q11–15)
11.C—"very large / great" — "Enormous" means extremely large or great in size or importance.
12.B—"selfish" — "Generous" means willing to give freely; the opposite is "selfish" (caring only about oneself).
13.A—"predicts" — A weather forecast "predicts" (says what will happen in the future). "Prevents" means to stop something.
14.D—"ancient" — "Dating back over 2,000 years" tells us the ruins are very old. "Ancient" means belonging to the very distant past.
15.C—"reminded" — To "remind" someone means to cause them to remember something they might forget. It fits the context best.
Section C: Error Correction (Q16–20)
16.A—"have" should be "has." With the subject "She" (third person singular), Present Perfect uses "has been working," not "have."
17.A—"was playing" should be "were playing." The subject "The children" is plural, so the verb must be "were," not "was."
18.A—"the most tallest" is incorrect — double superlative. "Tall" is a one-syllable adjective, so the correct form is simply "the tallest."
19.A—"will have" should be "have." In a First Conditional if-clause, use Present Simple (not will). → "If I have enough money…"
20.B—"to visit" should be "visiting." The verb "enjoy" is always followed by a gerund (verb + -ing), not an infinitive.
Section D: Reading Comprehension — Passage 1 (Q21–25)
21.B—The passage discusses how technology has changed communication while also warning about its downsides — use it wisely.
22.C—The passage states that excessive screen time can "disrupt sleep patterns" — this is a negative effect mentioned directly.
23.D—"Excessive" means more than is reasonable or necessary — in other words, "too much."
24.B—The passage says "a reply could take days or even weeks to arrive" when people used handwritten letters.
25.C—The passage concludes: "it is essential to use it wisely and maintain a healthy balance between the digital and offline worlds."
Section D: Reading Comprehension — Passage 2 (Q26–30)
26.B—The passage explains the Amazon is called "lungs of the Earth" because it "absorbs carbon dioxide and releases oxygen" — like lungs breathing.
27.D—The passage defines deforestation as "the clearing of forests for farming, logging, and urban development."
28.A—"Vital" means absolutely necessary or extremely important. The Amazon plays a "critical role" — so "extremely important" fits perfectly.
29.D—The passage says the Amazon "affects rainfall patterns and temperatures worldwide" — so its destruction could cause serious climate problems globally.
30.C—The last sentence calls Amazon's destruction "not only a local tragedy but a global crisis" — the writer believes everyone must take responsibility.
