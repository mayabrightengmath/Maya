Write these integers in order from smallest to largest:

Working

Number N has exactly 8 factors; two are 6 and 10. What could N be?
5 marks
Working

6=2×3, 10=2×5 → N divisible by 2, 3, 5

Answer

18
Calculate:  a) √(4³ + 2×12 − 4²)   b) (−3)² − 2³ + 5×(−2)
6 marks
Working

a) 4³=64, 2×12=24, 4²=16 → 64+24−16=72 → √72 = 6√2 ≈ 8.49

b) (−3)²=+9, 2³=8, 5×(−2)=−10 → 9−8−10 = −9

Answer
a) 6√2 (≈ 8.49)     b) −9
Common error in b): (−3)² = −9. Squaring any real number gives a positive result.
