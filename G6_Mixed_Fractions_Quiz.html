Let's Recall

Fraction Operations

➕ Adding Fractions

To add fractions with the same denominator, add the numerators and keep the denominator.

Same Denominator

27 + 37 = 2+37 = 57

For different denominators, find the Lowest Common Denominator (LCD) first, then convert and add.

Different Denominators — Find LCM(3,4) = 12
Step 1 — Convert to same denominator
13 = 412     14 = 312
Step 2 — Add
412 + 312 = 712
➖ Subtracting Fractions

To subtract fractions with different denominators, find the common denominator first, convert, then subtract the numerators.

Example — LCM(4,6) = 12
Step 1 — Convert
34 = 912     16 = 212
Step 2 — Subtract
912 − 212 = 712
✖️ Multiplying Fractions

Multiply the numerators together, then multiply the denominators together. Simplify if possible.

Example
Multiply numerators: 2 × 4 = 8  |  Multiply denominators: 3 × 5 = 15
23 × 45 = 2 × 43 × 5 = 815

💡 "of" means multiply!   e.g. "half of 34" means 12 × 34

➗ Dividing Fractions

Use the Keep–Change–Flip method:

🔒
KEEP
Keep the first fraction
🔄
CHANGE
÷ becomes ×
🔃
FLIP
Flip the second fraction
Example
Apply Keep–Change–Flip
23 ÷ 45 = 23 × 54 = 1012
Simplify ÷ 2
1012 = 56
🔢 Mixed Numbers

A mixed number has a whole part and a fraction part. Example: 213

To calculate with mixed numbers, first convert to an improper fraction:

Converting Mixed Number → Improper Fraction
Formula: (whole × denominator) + numerator, over the same denominator
213 = (2 × 3) + 13 = 73
More Examples

112 = 32     325 = 175     234 = 114

📋 Order of Operations — BIDMAS

When an expression has more than one operation, always follow this order:

( )
Brackets
x²
Indices
✖️➗
× and ÷
➕➖
+ and −

⚠️ Important: Multiplication and division are done left to right. Addition and subtraction are also done left to right.

Example — 1/2 + 2/3 × 3/4
Step 1 — Multiply first (BIDMAS)
23 × 34 = 612 = 12
Step 2 — Then add
12 + 12 = 1

Study Tips

How to Solve Mixed Fraction Operations

1

Check the operation first

Look carefully at whether the question uses +, −, ×, or ÷. Don't rush — each operation has a different method.

2

Convert mixed numbers first

If the question has mixed numbers like 213, always convert them to improper fractions before calculating.

3

Same denominator for + and −

For addition and subtraction, the denominators must be the same. Find the LCM of the denominators to create an equivalent fraction.

4

Multiply across for ×

For multiplication, just multiply numerators together and denominators together. Simplify if possible — you can even cross-cancel before multiplying!

5

Keep–Change–Flip for ÷

For division: Keep the first fraction → Change ÷ to × → Flip the second fraction. Then multiply normally.

6

Follow BIDMAS always

When a problem has more than one operation, always do × and ÷ before + and −. Brackets are done first of all.

7

Simplify your answer

Always check if your fraction can be simplified. Divide the numerator and denominator by their HCF. Convert improper fractions to mixed numbers if asked.

8

Read word problems carefully

Underline important numbers and keywords. The keyword tells you which operation to use.

altogether · total · combined
→ Addition ➕
left · remaining · how much more
→ Subtraction ➖
of · times · product
→ Multiplication ✖️
shared equally · per · each · split
→ Division ➗

Real-World Maths

Math in Real Life

Fractions appear everywhere in daily life — here are some examples you might encounter.

🍳

Cooking & Recipes

Recipes often use fractions for measurements like cups, teaspoons, and tablespoons.

A recipe needs 34 cup of milk. Making half the recipe:
12 × 34 = 38 cup
🍕

Sharing Food

When sharing pizza, cake, or other food, we use fractions to describe each person's share.

A pizza is shared equally among 6 people. Each person gets 16 of the pizza.
🧴

Measuring Liquid

Liquid amounts in bottles, glasses, and containers are often described using fractions.

A bottle has 34 L of juice. After drinking 12 L:
34 − 12 = 14 L left
🎀

Cutting Rope or Ribbon

When crafting or wrapping, we often cut ribbon into fractional lengths.

You have 212 m of ribbon. You use 114 m:
212 − 114 = 114 m left
🛍️

Shopping Discounts

Discounts are often fractions of the original price (e.g., 14 off).

A shirt costs ฿240. Sale is 14 off:
Discount = 14 × 240 = ฿60 off
⏱️

Time & Distance

Journey times and distances are often expressed as fractions of an hour or kilometre.

A journey takes 34 hour. After 14 hour:
34 − 14 = 24 = 12 hour left
⚽

Sports & Practice

Practice schedules and game statistics often use fractions to describe time and performance.

A player trains for 112 hours, 4 days a week:
Total = 112 × 4 = 6 hours
