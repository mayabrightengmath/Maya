15
Calculate:
a) −6 + (−4)    b) −3 × 5    c) −24 ÷ (−6)    d) (−2)³
6 marks

📐 Working

a) −6 + (−4) = −6 − 4 = −10   [+ negative → subtract]

b) −3 × 5 = −15   [positive × negative = negative]

c) −24 ÷ (−6) = +4   [negative ÷ negative = positive]

d) (−2)³ = (−2)×(−2)×(−2) = 4×(−2) = −8

✅
Answer
a) −10    b) −15    c) 4    d) −8
📝 Common error: Students may write (−2)³ = +8. Remind them: negative × negative = positive, then positive × negative = NEGATIVE. An odd power of a negative number is always negative.

A number N has exactly 8 factors. Two of its factors are 6 and 10. What could N be? Explain your reasoning.
5 marks

📐 Working

6 = 2 × 3   and   10 = 2 × 5  →  N must be divisible by 2, 3, and 5

Smallest such N = LCM(6, 10) = 30

Factors of 30: 1, 2, 3, 5, 6, 10, 15, 30  →  exactly 8 factors ✓

✅
Answer
N = 30

18
Calculate (without calculator):
a) √(4³ + 2 × 12 − 4²)     b) (−3)² − 2³ + 5 × (−2)
6 marks

📐 Working

a) Inside the root:

   4³ = 64    2 × 12 = 24    4² = 16

   64 + 24 − 16 = 72

   √72 = √(36 × 2) = 6√2 ≈ 8.49

b) Apply BIDMAS:

   (−3)² = +9    [brackets first → even power → positive]

   2³ = 8

   5 × (−2) = −10

   9 − 8 + (−10) = 9 − 8 − 10 = −9

✅
Answer
a) 6√2  (or √72 ≈ 8.49)     b) −9
📝 Common error b): Students write (−3)² = −9. Remind: brackets must be applied FIRST, and squaring a negative gives a POSITIVE result.

Find THREE numbers with HCF = 4 and LCM = 120. Show that your answer is correct.
5 marks

📐 Working

LCM = 120 = 2³ × 3 × 5    HCF = 4 = 2²

All three must: (i) be multiples of 4    (ii) divide into 120

Candidates: 4, 8, 12, 20, 24, 40, 60, 120

Try 4, 24, 40:

   HCF(4, 24, 40): GCF = 4 ✓  (8 does not divide 4)

   LCM(4, 24, 40): 24 = 2³×3, 40 = 2³×5 → LCM = 2³×3×5 = 120 ✓

✅
Answer (accept any valid set)
4, 24, 40  →  HCF = 4 ✓    LCM = 120 ✓
📝 Teacher note: Accept any correct set (e.g. 4, 8, 120 also works). Deduct 2 marks if student gives numbers but shows no verification of HCF and LCM.
