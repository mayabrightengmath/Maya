What is a Multiple?

A multiple is the result of multiplying a number by 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, and so on. Multiples of a number go on forever — they never stop!

✅ Quick Check

Is 20 a multiple of 4?

4 × 5 = 20 ✓   Yes! Because 4 divides exactly into 20 with no remainder.

Is 14 a multiple of 4?

14 ÷ 4 = 3 remainder 2   ✗   No! There is a remainder, so 14 is NOT a multiple of 4.

What is the LCM?

The Lowest Common Multiple (LCM) is the smallest number that appears in the multiple lists of two or more numbers. It is the first multiple that both numbers share.

Worked Example — Find the LCM of 4 and 6
Step 1 — List multiples of 4
4
8
12
16
20
24
…
Step 2 — List multiples of 6
6
12
18
24
30
…
Step 3 — Find common multiples (appear in BOTH lists)
12
24
…

Common multiples of 4 and 6 are: 12, 24, 36, …

Step 4 — Choose the LOWEST (smallest) common multiple
12

∴ LCM of 4 and 6 = 12

Study Tips

How to Find the LCM

1

List multiples carefully

Start with the smaller number and list its multiples. Look for the first number that appears in both lists. Don't skip any multiples or you might miss the LCM!

2

Choose the LOWEST common multiple

Do not pick just any common multiple. The LCM must be the smallest common multiple. For example, 12 and 24 are both common multiples of 4 and 6, but the LCM is 12.

3

If one number is a multiple of the other

If one number divides exactly into the other, the larger number is automatically the LCM — no listing needed!

Example

LCM of 5 and 10: Since 10 = 5 × 2, the LCM is simply 10.

4

Use prime factorisation for bigger numbers

For large numbers, listing every multiple takes too long. Instead, use prime factorisation:

Worked Example — LCM of 12 and 18
Step 1 — Prime factorise each number

12 = 2² × 3     18 = 2 × 3²

Step 2 — Take the HIGHEST power of each prime

Highest power of 2: 2²    Highest power of 3: 3²

Step 3 — Multiply

LCM = 2² × 3² = 4 × 9 = 36

∴ LCM of 12 and 18 = 36

5

Spot word problems using LCM

In word problems, look for repeated events or cycles. Key words that mean you need LCM:

"every … seconds/days" "flash again together" "ring at the same time" "repeat together"
Example

Two buses depart every 4 and 6 minutes. LCM(4, 6) = 12 → they depart together every 12 minutes.
