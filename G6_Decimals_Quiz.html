🔢 Place Value & Fractions ↔ Decimals

Fractions and decimals can show the same value. The decimal point separates whole numbers from decimal parts.

Key Equivalences

1/100 = 0.01
1/2 = 0.5
1/4 = 0.25

🔄 Converting Fractions and Decimals

A. Fraction → Decimal

Method 1: Divide the numerator by the denominator.

Example
3/4 = 3 ÷ 4 = 0.75

Method 2: Change the denominator to 10, 100, or 1000 if possible.

Examples
7/10 = 0.7
23/100 = 0.23

B. Decimal → Fraction

Write the decimal over 10, 100, or 1000 (based on decimal places), then simplify.

Examples
1 decimal place → over 10
0.6 = 6/10 = 3/5
2 decimal places → over 100
0.25 = 25/100 = 1/4
3 decimal places → over 1000
0.125 = 125/1000 = 1/8
➕➖ Addition and Subtraction of Decimals

When adding or subtracting decimals, always line up the decimal points. Add zeros if needed to make columns even.

Addition — 3.45 + 2.6
3 . 4 5
2 . 6 0
6 . 0 5
Subtraction — 8.2 − 3.75
8 . 2 0
3 . 7 5
4 . 4 5

⚠️ Key Tip: Write zeros after the decimal to fill empty places before calculating. This avoids careless errors!

✖️ Multiplication of Decimals

Step 1: Multiply as if they are whole numbers (ignore decimal points).
Step 2: Count the total decimal places in both numbers.
Step 3: Put the decimal point in your answer.

Example 1 — 2.4 × 0.3
Ignore decimal: 24 × 3 = 72
Count decimal places: 2.4 has 1, 0.3 has 1 → total = 2
Place decimal 2 places from right
Answer = 0.72
Example 2 — 1.25 × 4
Ignore decimal: 125 × 4 = 500
Decimal places: 1.25 has 2, 4 has 0 → total = 2
Answer
1.25 × 4 = 5.00 = 5

🔑 Multiplying by 10 moves the decimal point 1 place right. Multiplying by 100 moves it 2 places right.

➗ Division of Decimals

When dividing by a decimal, make the divisor a whole number by multiplying both numbers by 10, 100, etc.

Example 1 — 4.8 ÷ 0.6 (divisor has 1 decimal place → multiply by 10)
Multiply both by 10
4.8 × 10 = 48  |  0.6 × 10 = 6
48 ÷ 6 = 8
So 4.8 ÷ 0.6 = 8
Example 2 — 6.3 ÷ 3 (divisor is already whole)
Divide directly
6.3 ÷ 3 = 2.1

🔑 Dividing by 10 moves the decimal point 1 place left. Dividing by 100 moves it 2 places left.

Avoid Mistakes

How to Avoid Common Mistakes

1

Line up decimal points for + and −

For addition and subtraction, always align the decimal points in a column before calculating.

2

Don't line up decimals for multiplication

For multiplication, ignore the decimal, multiply whole numbers first, then count total decimal places and place the point.

3

Make the divisor whole for division

If dividing by a decimal like 0.6, multiply both numbers by 10 first to make 0.6 into 6.

4

Add zeros when needed

4.5 can be written as 4.50. Adding zeros after the last decimal digit does not change the value but helps with column work.

5

Estimate first

Before calculating, estimate to check your answer makes sense. For example, 2.9 + 3.1 ≈ 3 + 3 = 6, so an answer of 60 would be wrong.

6

Remember place values for conversion

0.7 = 7/10 0.07 = 7/100 0.007 = 7/1000
